Update the investment engine by adding a Damodaran-Informed Value Driver Engine before the Signal Prioritization Engine.

Purpose:
The system should not prioritize events only because they look positive or negative. It should prioritize based on whether the event changes intrinsic value drivers.

Core valuation logic:
Every event, SEC filing, uploaded report, or company update must be evaluated across the following Damodaran-style value drivers:

1. Cash Flow Impact
- Does the event improve or weaken operating cash flows?
- Is the impact temporary or recurring?
- Is the company moving closer to sustainable free cash flow?

2. Growth Quality
- Is revenue growth accelerating or decelerating?
- Is growth supported by fundamentals or just narrative?
- Is growth value-creating or value-destroying?

3. Reinvestment Efficiency
- Is the company reinvesting effectively?
- Are R&D, CAPEX, acquisitions, or working capital producing better growth?
- If reinvestment rises without better growth, flag as negative.

4. Margin Trajectory
- Are operating margins improving, stable, or compressing?
- Is margin improvement structural or one-off?
- Compare current margin direction with previous filings or uploaded reports.

5. Cost of Capital / Risk
- Does the event increase business risk, financial risk, regulatory risk, or country risk?
- Does leverage, uncertainty, litigation, cyclicality, or macro sensitivity increase?
- If risk rises, increase discount-rate pressure.

6. Competitive Advantage / Moat
- Does the event strengthen or weaken pricing power, market share, switching costs, data advantage, regulatory moat, reimbursement access, or brand strength?
- Separate real moat from management claims.

7. Narrative vs Numbers Consistency
- Compare management narrative with actual numbers.
- If management tone is positive but numbers deteriorate, flag narrative risk.
- If numbers confirm the thesis, increase confidence.

8. Valuation Gap / Repricing Risk
- Decide whether the event is likely to change market expectations.
- Classify repricing probability as Low / Medium / High.
- Distinguish accounting noise from true value-driver change.

Output required for each analyzed document or event:

A. Damodaran Value Driver Summary
- Cash Flow Impact: Positive / Neutral / Negative
- Growth Quality: Improving / Stable / Weakening
- Reinvestment Efficiency: Improving / Stable / Weakening / Unknown
- Margin Trajectory: Improving / Stable / Weakening
- Cost of Capital Pressure: Low / Medium / High
- Moat Direction: Strengthening / Stable / Weakening
- Narrative vs Numbers: Aligned / Mixed / Diverging
- Repricing Risk: Low / Medium / High

B. Valuation Impact
Classify:
- Value Accretive
- Neutral
- Value Destructive
- Unclear / Low Confidence

C. Reasoning
Provide 3-5 concise reasons grounded in evidence from the filing or uploaded document.

D. Evidence Requirement
Every valuation conclusion must include:
- source section if available
- extracted metric or quoted text
- confidence level

E. Feed into prioritization
Create a future-ready priority formula:

Priority Score =
Thesis Impact × Valuation Impact × Risk Change × Confidence × Portfolio Weight

Do not build a full DCF yet.
Keep it rule-based, lightweight, and explainable.
